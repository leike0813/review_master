\begin{abstract}
Sequence transduction models are usually built from recurrent or convolutional components together with attention. We study a simpler architecture, the Transformer, that uses attention throughout the encoder and decoder. Beyond improved parallelism, the key benefit is that self-attention shortens the path length between distant positions, allowing the model to exchange long-range information without recurrent state updates or stacked convolutional receptive fields. On WMT 2014 English-to-German and English-to-French translation, the model achieves stronger BLEU scores than competitive baselines while training faster under the stated hardware and FLOPs accounting convention. The results support attention-centric sequence transduction on these benchmarks, while leaving broader generalization claims to future work.
\end{abstract}

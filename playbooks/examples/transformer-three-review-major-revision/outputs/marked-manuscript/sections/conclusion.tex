\section{Conclusion}

We introduced the Transformer, an attention-based sequence transduction model that removes recurrence and convolution from the core architecture. Across standard machine translation benchmarks, the model reaches state-of-the-art quality with strong computational efficiency under the reported hardware and cost assumptions.

\replaced{These findings suggest that attention-based modeling is a strong foundation for large-scale translation. Claims beyond that scope should be made cautiously: the present evidence is strongest for the benchmark tasks studied here, and broader modality or sequence-length generalization remains future work.}{These findings suggest that attention-based modeling may become a general foundation for many sequence-processing tasks beyond the translation setting studied here. We expect this line of work to remain broadly useful as larger datasets and broader modalities are considered.}

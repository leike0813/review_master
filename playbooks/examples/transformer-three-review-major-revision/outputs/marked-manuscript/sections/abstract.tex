\begin{abstract}
Sequence transduction models are usually built from recurrent or convolutional components together with attention. We study a simpler architecture, the Transformer, that uses attention throughout the encoder and decoder. \added{Beyond improved parallelism, the key benefit is that self-attention shortens the path length between distant positions, allowing the model to exchange long-range information without recurrent state updates or stacked convolutional receptive fields.} On WMT 2014 English-to-German and English-to-French translation, the model achieves stronger BLEU scores than competitive baselines while training faster under the stated hardware and FLOPs accounting convention. \replaced{The results support attention-centric sequence transduction on these benchmarks, while leaving broader generalization claims to future work.}{The results suggest that attention-centric architectures are a promising alternative for machine translation and possibly for other sequence modeling tasks.}
\end{abstract}
